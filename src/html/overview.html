Overview

The experience I like most when it comes to programming and scripting is you will never stop learning. The Boring Stuff...

Well, I was born at... Ha! you thought I was going to go way back. No I'll start with the basis of how I've got where I am today.

In highschool I wasn't the most popular kid nor the nerd. I was your typical adverage teenager. Worked hard to keep my grades at a standard my parents set and hung out with my friends when I could. Found the occasional trouble from acting before thinking but you get the drift. In these days I did play alot of Diablo and StarCraft on my mothers old Windows 98. Held up like a champ she did. Hell, I think it wasn't until Windows 7 that my mother finally retired that desk rock. Either way, in these days I was continuously being told by my parents to strive towads technology because I seemed to pick up on it rather quickly. Now to move forward to after High School.

I didn't take the scholarship route as most of my classmates did. Instead I choose to pickup arms and join the United States military. The War on Terrorism was the propoganda at the time. Seemed right. Thought I'd serve my country and contribute to society. Turns out I found myself working on the KC-135 StratoTanker for the United States Air Force. 6 years of turning wrenches and never eneding refuels. It was a great experience. Was able to see parts of the world I have yet to re-experience thus far. If I had to give a suggestion on this for those looking into the United States Air Force, Do it. It will have it's sucky moments, but so is life. It's a great oportunity and thus far the oportunity has givith me further oportunities since I got out. Just take my word and go nonner life. You'll thank me later.

Since I have left the military with my small little family and found myself working as a technician for WalMart. Glad I took the route I did. When I was first looking into work I had a logistics career field in mind. It's what I knew at the time and had since forgot how the feel of the keyboard felt amungst my fingertips. Luckyly a special someone guided me towards the technology side of WalMart and I've been doing this ever since. For about a year I assisted users with their software issues. Doing it the long way to correct issues. Then I saw a co-worker using PowerShell. He built scripts to automate his job to make his life easier. Thats where I found myself scripting in PowerShell. Since, I have found myself developing scripts for my team as well as working within our internal teams development subgroup helping maintain and add to our internal Self-Service website lowering call volumn and allowing for more time on completing the issues comming through.
